About Acne

Acne is a skin condition that occurs when oil and dead skin cells clog up your pores causing pimples, whiteheads, or blackheads. It can occur in people of all ages but is most common during puberty, when sebaceous glands activate, producing oil.

Acne is the most common skin condition in the United States, affecting up to 50 million Americans each year. Though it is not dangerous, it can be uncomfortable and may leave scars.

Types of Acne

  • Blackheads: Open, clogged pores with air exposure causing discoloration.
  • Whiteheads: Closed pores with a white tip.
  • Papules: Small, red bumps caused by clogged pores.
  • Pustules (or pimples): Small red bumps filled with pus.
  • Nodules: Firm, skin-colored bumps deep under the skin.
  • Cysts: Large, inflamed, and painful lesions beneath the skin.

Acne Causes

Acne occurs when pores on the skin's surface become clogged by hair follicles, oily skin, bacteria, and dead skin cells. Hormonal changes, medications, makeup, air pollution, weather, and clothing can be common triggers.

Acne Complications

Severe acne breakouts can lead to acne scars, which can become more pronounced with age. The American Academy of Dermatology recommends clearing skin of acne breakouts prior to undergoing treatment for acne scars.
